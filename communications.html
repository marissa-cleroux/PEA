Research Report

As a capstone research project in the Computer Science program, each student was assigned to complete a research report on a topic of their choice. For my topic I chose Ethical Hacking. Ethical Hacking is a thriving Information Technology (I.T.) industry where in companies pay hackers to attempt to break into their systems. Ethical hacking enables businesses to discover vulnerabilities in their system before malicious actors have the chance to.

To complete the research report, I first gathered sources each week leading up to the due date - ensuring the sources were credible. After familiarizing myself with the topic I created an outline that had to be approved by my professor. From there, I drafted and completed the report.

The report covers the different types of hackers, the history of hacking, the pros and cons of being an ethical hacker, how ethical hackers hack, and career paths in ethical hacking. In the new program, the Computer Science program has integrated ethical hacking into its curriculum and students already learn about common exploits and how to protect against them. I suggested that teachers can incentivise students to perform ethical hacking on their projects by allotting a certain percentage of marks to security that the students will lose if they fail to protect against common attacks. I also suggest that students should be educated on the risks of being an ethical hacker. For more details read the report here or review the presentation here.
