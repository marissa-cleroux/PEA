Requirements { Objective 5 }

Business Problem

The Nursing Program has a series of skills that students are required to practice in the lab setting and then in the clinical setting after successfully practicing it in the lab setting. Before Nursing Skills Tracker (NST), students had to track their skills via paper documents throughout the course of the program. It was very difficult for teachers to determine when students could practice a skill in a clinical setting. Students also desired a way to generate a portfolio of the skills they had acquired. Teachers also wanted a way for students to keep track of when it’s time to practice a skill again so that student can be autonomous. NST solved all this by allowing students and teachers to input evaluations for skills and easily show which skills students have practiced. The original elevator pitch for NST can be found here.

Scope

Users & Other Systems

NST has several categories of users:

  • Nursing Department Coordinator
  • Nursing Teachers
  • Nursing Clinical Teachers (teachers who supervise students in the clinical setting)
  • Nursing Liaison Teachers (teachers who act as liaisons for students in their preceptorship)
  • Nursing Students

NST depends on two other systems

  • Clara (an interface which stores all student, teacher, and program data)
  • The Heritage College Application Management System (AMS) (manages applications and authorization for Heritage College applications deployed by the Computer Science program)

Functionality

Coordinator Functionality

  • Add, remove, and update units
  • Add, remove, and update skills
  • View all students in the program
  • Evaluate lab and clinical skills for all students in the program
  • Assign students to clinical teachers
  • Assign students to liaison teachers
  • Generate portfolios for past and current students
  • View summaries of skills learned by all students in the program

Teacher, Liaison, and Clinical Teacher Functionality

  • View all students in their cohort
  • Evaluate lab and clinical skills for students in their cohort
  • View student’s self-evaluations for lab and clinical skills
  • View previous evaluations for student’s lab and clinical skills
  • View summaries of skills learned by student’s in their cohort

Student Functionality

  • Evaluate their own lab and clinical skills
  • View teacher’s evaluations for lab and clinical skills
  • View previous evaluations for lab and clinical skills
  • Generate portfolio of skills acquired

Business Requirements

Business requirements are requirements for a system from the perspective of the business. A functional requirement describes something that the system must do. A non-functional requirement describes how the system must be. All functional requirements are captured by user stories, whereas non-functional requirements are usually captured in the acceptance criteria of those user stories. An example of a non-functional requirement in NST is to optimize the GetAllSkills() method so that it is faster than 18 seconds.

Based on the initial presentation by the client the team summarized the perceived requirements. We then discussed with the client if these requirements covered what the she wanted and what her priorities were. We also took the opportunity to clarify any confusing specifications and refine the requirements. That meeting is summarized in these meeting minutes. After gathering the initial requirements, we implemented some functionality based on the client’s priorities and the time allocated to complete the sprint. During the sprint review meeting, the client provided feedback and had the final say on whether the requirements were met or if more work was needed; the notes are foundhere . The same process was then carried out for the second sprint, as summarized here.

Example Requirement

As an example, one user story that I was the main contributor for is: “As a student, I want to be able to update when I have practiced a skill.” Before this functionality was complete, a student was not able to add a new evaluation to a skill once they had evaluated. The client wanted the student to be able to use NST to track when they should practice a skill again, and thus be able to update their evaluation every time they practice it.
